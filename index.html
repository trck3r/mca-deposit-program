Quick answers.

The honest answers to the questions every prospect asks before their first call.

"This sounds too good to be true."
We hear this on every single call. The math is consistent with the entire MCA industry — not unique to us. OnDeck reports 46% portfolio yields in public 10-K filings. Merchants pay 20-40% factor rates because banks won't fund them in the timeline they need (48 hours vs 90 days). We earn the spread between what the business pays and what you receive. Each monthly return comes from real ACH withdrawals on real businesses — verifiable in your dashboard.
"What if the business doesn't pay?"
Four protective layers. First-position UCC liens on receivables, equipment, and inventory — we get paid before any other creditor. ACH access on merchant processor reserves — $10-15K accessible immediately if needed. 7-30 day contract window gives operations room to collect before any "default" is called. Over-collateralized deployment — we lend $20K into businesses with $100K+ in seizable assets. In the program's history, no lender has lost principal.
"How quickly can I get my money out?"
At the end of each monthly cycle, your position closes. You decide on the spot — redeploy into the next cycle, or wire it back to your account. Maximum lock-up is 30 days. Once we hit our $2M cap and switch to line-of-credit fulfillment, existing participants get accelerated liquidity options.
"Why isn't everyone doing this?"
They are — at the institutional level. CFG Merchant Solutions raised $145M from institutional lenders in 2024. American Express paid $850M for Kabbage. Private equity owns most of this market. Once operators hit scale ($30M+ lines of credit), they stop needing individual participants entirely. What's rare is access to a smaller book that's still open. We're capping ours at ~$2M before we close to new participants.
"Do I need to be an accredited investor?"
No. This is private lending — not a securities offering. We purchase future receivables. The contract uses "lender" and "borrower" language, not "investor" or "investment." This isn't a workaround; it's the standard legal structure for the entire MCA industry.
"Why does the program cap at $2M?"
Once we hit $2M in deployed capital with a clean track record, we qualify for institutional credit lines. At that point, we self-fund and don't need new participants. Existing lenders get rolled into a more flexible structure with better liquidity. Getting in now means getting in before the book closes permanently.
